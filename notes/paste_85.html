Plain Paste


Teachers often make good use of web-page resources by copying them and pasting them into their lessons.


Unfortunately, when the copied text is pasted, it also brings with it the format used on the web-page.


Now you can quickly paste text into your word document, and keep the format you are using by pressing:


WindowsKey and V

ALTERNATE SHORTCUT:

ScrollLock Key and V


Swap Paste


Sometimes you have something on your clipboard that you want to paste into your document, but at the same time you want to make a swap and remove the replaced text to place it somewhere else.


This can be time-consuming and messy.


The Teacher Toolkit has a built in swap for pasting - highlight the text you want to swap and press:


WindowsKey and X

ALTERNATE SHORTCUT:

ScrollLock Key and X


This will paste your clipboard, and at the same time, remove the highlighted text and place it onto your clipboard.


For example:


You have a document containing the words                                Year 9 maths

You want to replace this with the words                                        Year 10 science

And move the Year 9 maths into a different document.


Place the Year 10 science onto your clipboard.

Highlight the Year 9 maths and press WindowsKey and X


The Year 9 maths will be replaced with Year 10 science and your clipboard now contains Year 9 maths ready to be pasted elsewhere.
